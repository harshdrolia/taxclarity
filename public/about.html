About TaxClarity

Tax information shouldn't require a subscription or a law degree

Professional tax research lives behind four-figure paywalls. Free tax content is mostly SEO sludge that's afraid to cite anything. TaxClarity is the third option: rigorous, sourced, plain-English, and free — for the student decoding a 1040-NR, the founder with their first K-1, and the professional who needs a fast, cited starting point.

Editorial standards — how we stay accountable

Sourced or it doesn't ship

Every figure traces to a primary source: the Code, Treasury regulations, Revenue Procedures (TY2025: Rev. Proc. 2024-40; TY2026: Rev. Proc. 2025-32), and IRS releases — as modified by OBBBA (P.L. 119-21). Where we simplify, we say so on the page.

Dated and versioned

Content carries a last-reviewed date. Tax law changes; a page that doesn't tell you when it was checked is a page you can't trust. Current data version: v3.0 · reviewed July 2026.

AI is labeled and linked

The research agent's memos are AI-generated and marked as such. Its citations are converted to links server-side precisely so you can catch its mistakes — verification is the product, not an afterthought.

Corrections welcome

Found an error? Use the contact form below. Substantiated corrections get fixed and acknowledged. That's how a Wikipedia-grade resource is supposed to work.

How it's built

Node.js/Express backend with a pure-function tax engine (TY2025 + TY2026), a curated authority directory powering site search, and a Claude-based research agent behind a secured server-side proxy — the API key never touches your browser, and every citation is linkified deterministically (Cornell LII for statute and regs, IRS.gov for forms and pubs) rather than trusted from model output. Static front-end, no trackers, no ads.

Disclaimer, privacy & terms

Not advice. TaxClarity provides general educational information about U.S. federal tax law. Nothing here — including calculator output and AI research memos — is tax, legal, accounting, or investment advice, and nothing creates a client relationship. Rules turn on facts; consult a qualified CPA, EA, or attorney about your situation before acting. Nothing on this site is intended or written to be used, and cannot be used, for the purpose of avoiding federal tax penalties.

Accuracy. We work hard to keep figures current (see editorial standards above) but make no warranty of completeness or fitness for a particular purpose. Primary sources control; that's why we link them.

Privacy. No ads, no trackers, no sale of data. Calculator inputs are processed to compute your draft and are not stored. Research questions are processed through Anthropic's API to generate memos and are subject to Anthropic's data policies; don't include names, SSNs, or identifying details. The newsletter stores only your email, used only to send updates; every email includes an opt-out, honored immediately. Contact submissions are used solely to respond.

Terms. Free for personal and professional use. Don't scrape abusively, hammer the API, or republish wholesale without attribution. Templates are provided as-is, free, for your own engagements. External links (Cornell LII, IRS.gov, CourtListener) belong to their owners; TaxClarity is unaffiliated with them and with the IRS.
