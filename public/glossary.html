Glossary

Finance Terms, Plain English

Every confusing term you've encountered — explained like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook.

AGI
Adjusted Gross Income — your total income minus specific deductions like IRA contributions or student loan interest. Used to calculate many tax benefits.
Example: $60,000 income − $3,000 IRA contribution = $57,000 AGI.
W-2
Form your employer sends by January 31 showing wages earned and taxes withheld. You need this to file your 2025 return.
Box 1 = taxable wages. Box 2 = federal tax withheld.
1099
Form for income NOT from an employer — freelance work, bank interest, dividends, crypto. No taxes withheld, so you may owe more at filing.
Client paid you $2,500 for work → they send 1099-NEC.
FICA
Federal Insurance Contributions Act — 7.65% taken from every paycheck: 6.2% Social Security + 1.45% Medicare. Your employer matches it.
On a $4,000 paycheck: $306 goes to FICA taxes.
OBBB
One Big Beautiful Bill — major tax legislation signed July 4, 2025. Raised standard deductions, boosted Child Tax Credit to $2,200, raised SALT cap to $40,000, eliminated tax on qualifying tips and overtime.
Affects all 2025 tax returns filed in 2026.
§199A / QBI
Qualified Business Income deduction — allows pass-through business owners (S-Corps, partnerships, sole proprietors) to deduct 20% of qualified business income. Made permanent by OBBB in 2025.
$100,000 in QBI → $20,000 deduction → saves ~$4,400 in taxes at 22%.
SALT Cap
State and Local Tax deduction cap — limits how much state income/property tax you can deduct on your federal return. Was $10,000. Raised to $40,000 for 2025–2029 by OBBB.
NY taxpayer paying $35,000 in state taxes can now deduct the full amount.
Roth IRA
Retirement account funded with after-tax money. Grows tax-free; withdrawals in retirement are 100% tax-free. 2025 limit: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+).
$7,000 at age 25 could grow to $100,000+ by retirement — all tax-free.
Capital Gains
Profit from selling an investment. Short-term (under 1 year): taxed as regular income. Long-term (over 1 year): taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20%. 2025: 0% rate applies up to $48,350 single.
Bought stock at $10, sold at $25 after 2 years → $15 long-term capital gain.
ITIN
Individual Taxpayer Identification Number — 9-digit number (starts with 9) for people who need to file taxes but can't get a Social Security Number. Apply via Form W-7.
International student with no SSN → apply for ITIN to file Form 8843.
PTE Election
Pass-Through Entity tax election — available in most states, lets an S-Corp or partnership pay state income tax at the entity level. Shareholders/partners get a credit, effectively bypassing the federal SALT cap for business income.
Available in 36+ states including NY, CA, IL, NJ, TX, FL.
Substantial Presence Test
IRS formula to determine if an international person is a "resident alien" for US taxes. Count: 100% of current year days + 1/3 of last year + 1/6 of two years ago. Need 183+ days total.
F-1 students are exempt from this test for their first 5 calendar years in the US.
